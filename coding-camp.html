Introduction

"Programming and coding can be a very collaborative and creative venture, with students working together to solve a problem or create an action. All these skills extend far beyond the classroom." From: http://www.colorincolorado.org/.

In our camp, we plan to teach via interactive games, instead of traditional lectures because we want to let the kids not only learn but be engaged and have fun!

Summary

Our youth camp focus on the basic concepts of computer science, bridge a cognition between students and programming, and cultivate a good relationship in family and school through learning small projects. The goals are to breakdown some misconceptions about computer programming, and help students to understand the fundamentals in computer science, and have fun!

2018 Micro::Bit Coding Camp

Is coding always about sitting in front of a computer? It doesn't have to be! In this camp, our children will be learn how to create a real stopwatch, a compass, or even a rock paper scissor machine, within program that they make in camp!

Our students will learn:

  • Learn basic coding concepts and create programs
  • Run their programs into a mini-computer called micro:bit"!
  • Apply their skills in micro:bit in many real world use cases!
  • Learn how to code javascript on micro:bit

What's Micro::Bit?

micro:bit is a mini-computer that is designed for teaching children in elementary programming and how to build new things! It was originally designed by BBC for computer education in UK, but now available worldwide and have reached more than 1 million children! Code.org, Microsoft have created curriculum and tools to help children use micro:bit to learn programming and other science subjects.
